**Deep Bite** (딥바이트, _Dibbaiteu_) is a new Cardbot in _[Metal Cardbot S](</wiki/Metal_Cardbot_S> "Metal Cardbot S")_. He is known on Planet Machina as the "**Outlaw of the Sea** " (바다의 무법자라는, _Badaui Mubeobjalaneun_) due to his bestial appearance, but is actually a gentle soul and is also a friend of [Glober](</wiki/Glober> "Glober"). As his appearance was accepted by [Jun Grant](</wiki/Jun_Grant> "Jun Grant"), [Blue Cop](</wiki/Blue_Cop> "Blue Cop") and [Rock Crush](</wiki/Rock_Crush> "Rock Crush") while [Red Blitz](</wiki/Red_Blitz> "Red Blitz") accepted him for his underwater abilities, Deep Bite volunteered to be sealed and joined the Cardbots. After the final battle for the _[Speranza](</wiki/Speranza> "Speranza")_ , Deep Bite joined the _Speranza_ crew to pay his debt for biting Gigantrex's head, and to search for the Organizer that fell into the lake.

In _[Metal Cardbot W](</wiki/Metal_Cardbot_W> "Metal Cardbot W")_ , it was revealed that Deep Bite is a Wild Cardbot carrier and the Beast Virus was the source of his tooth ailment, which lead to [Soleil](</wiki/Soleil> "Soleil") abducting him in _[Capture](</wiki/Capture> "Capture")_ , but the latter chose to return him to Jun Grant in _[Again? The Mysterious Transfer Student](</wiki/Again%3F_The_Mysterious_Transfer_Student> "Again? The Mysterious Transfer Student")_.

## History

On the planet Machina, Deep Bite was born a Wild Cardbot, which made him ostracized by the Metal Cardbots. As a result, Deep Bite decided to act feral and attack anything and anyone on sight, gaining the nickname "Outlaw of the Sea". Despite his appearance, [Glober](</wiki/Glober> "Glober") befriended and treated him with respect until they were forced to leave their dying word before its destruction, partaking a long journey to the planet Earth. After arriving, Deep Bite sustained damage to his vehicle form's metal teeth, rendering him crazy and attacking anything in sight underwater. One day, he came across a freighter carrying [Gigantrex](</wiki/Gigantrex> "Gigantrex") sealed in a slab and sunk it, leading to Gigantrex's awakening before Deep Bite bit down into Gigantrex's head, infecting the latter with the Beast Virus that caused severe damage physically and mentally.

Episode 9: Deep Bite appears while attacking Crest and Sebastian, who discovered the Abyss while exploring the deep sea to find the 4th Metal Card. Glover escapes using the Thunder Whip while being dragged by Deep Bite's bite, but fails to capture him due to the sand cloud caused by Deep Bite. Deep Bite attacks Glober from above, breaking his helmet and causing him to suffocate. Crest rescues Glober, finding him stranded on a cliff near the shore.

After hearing Glober's story, the group gathers on the shore to plan a strategy. While alone, Peruru asks Glober about Deep Bite. Surprisingly, Glober reveals that Deep Bite was once his friend, similar to Rock Crush. According to Glober, though Deep Bite had a rough personality, he wasn’t violent or aggressive. Glober cannot understand why Deep Bite has become so ferocious. Meanwhile, Jun devises a plan to catch Deep Bite using Musclehyde's crane as a fishing rod and Red Blitz as bait. However, Deep Bite’s strength breaks the crane anchor, and Red Blitz barely escapes, injured and bitten. Theo notices that shards of Deep Bite’s teeth embedded in Red Blitz’s wounds are rusted and decayed, raising suspicion. As Theo plans a new operation, Red Blitz, angry about being used as bait, throws Jun into the sea.

Jun breaches the surface like a great white shark, grabs Peruru, and dives back in. Peruru uses Musclehyde’s crane as a jumping platform, stabs Deep Bite with her spear, and dives into the deep sea. Jun and Peruru initially think they’ve been eaten by Deep Bite, but Deep Bite surfaces and releases them. It’s revealed that Deep Bite has a cavity and rotten teeth, which caused the shards embedded in Red Blitz. Deep Bite reunites with Glober, and later visits Ed Motor to get his teeth repaired. Grateful, he promises to repay the favor. When Jun asks Deep Bite to join their team, he agrees readily, though Red Blitz hints at a brewing conflict by showing his reluctance.

Episode 14: When Red Blitz insults Deep Bite, calling him a fish, and brings up the story of biting Gigantrex's head, Deep Bite denies any memory of it, blaming his toothache. Red Blitz escalates the fight, claiming he’ll jog Deep Bite’s memory with a punch. Deep Bite, enraged, retaliates and blows Red Blitz away, leading to a full-blown brawl.

Due to their physical differences, the fight is one-sided, with Deep Bite dominating. While Red Blitz struggles, Jun uses a Metal Card setup to arm Red Blitz and orders them to fight to understand each other. Deep Bite also arms himself, but his Beam Trident is pierced by Red Blitz’s Crimson Shot. In response, Deep Bite blocks Red Blitz’s Scarlet Tornado with the Trident Submarine and even bites Red Blitz’s backside in the fog. When Cielo and Musclehyde protest that Deep Bite’s actions are excessive, he retorts they should be grateful he didn’t devour them whole, threatening them before leaving.

That evening, Jun and Blue Cop visit Deep Bite, expressing that he doesn’t seem as bad as they initially thought. Deep Bite shares his past, revealing he was shunned for his fearsome appearance and earned the nickname "Outlaw of the Sea." Encouraged by Rock Crush, who claims not to fear him, Deep Bite begins to feel more accepted. Later, Jun writes a script to reconcile Deep Bite and Red Blitz, but its absurdity leaves everyone doubting its effectiveness. The next day, during the reconciliation attempt, Rock Crush accidentally triggers the Goldbug device while slipping, which disables most Metal Cardbots and threatens the humans. Deep Bite steps in, using brute strength to stop the device, with Blue Cop and Red Blitz assisting. The situation is resolved, and Deep Bite, feeling happy, asks Jun to seal him while wagging his tail, having made peace with Red Blitz.

Episode 16: Upon seeing Blastrain go berserk, Deep Bite attempts to mediate, drawing from his experience of becoming violent. However, after being struck by Blastrain’s weapon, he becomes enraged and joins the battle. Using the Deep Trident and his shotgun, Deep Bite neutralizes Blastrain’s Blast Shot and temporarily subdues him in submarine form. Blastrain lifts and throws Deep Bite, before continuing the fight against Jun and Blue Cop.

Episode 18: When the Hidden Cardbot Spark Beat receives an interview from the Motown Times at the park on the Motown coast, Deep Bite goes into the lake behind the park and creates a tornadic waterspout with the Deep Trident's Trident Submarine, rides the waterspout, and appears in vehicle form, scaring the people to leave. Immediately after, he sprays water on Spark Beat and is cast back into the Metal Breath.

Episode 19: Deep Bite stayed behind at Edo Motors with Blastrain, Red Blitz, Rock Crush, Musclehyde and Cielo as a duel condition between Blue Cop and Spark Beat.

Episode 20: While sitting on the roof as the group converse about the Hidden Cardbots, Deep Bite says that he will secretly go to the Hidden Cardbots' base, an offshore oil field 10km south of the Motown coast, and blow it up, but Jun stops him because he does not want to fight the Hidden Cardbots. After that, he gathered together with the other Cardbots at Edo's warehouse to make-believe Kent commanding them with his own Metal Breath until Flash Vector abducted him. Deep Bite is then paralyzed along with Rock Crush, Red Blitz, and Musclehyde by Flash Vector's Machinasium Stinger darts, and casted back in the Metal Breath.

Episode 21: Deep Bite and Blastrain were summoned to help Blue Cop against the Spark Beat, Flash Vector and Sky Gallop to cover for Jun as he headed for the wounded Glober. Later, on Jun's orders, he attacks Spark Beat by transforming in vehicle mode to bite the Hidden Cardbot, but is hit by Gigantrex, who runs through Red Blitz, Musclehyde and Cielo, and sent both Deep Bite and Spark Beat flying through the air.

## Appearance

Deep Bite is an ultramarine-blue Wild Cardbot with a shark/diver motif, a head with one shark dorsal fin on the top and a fin on both the left and right side, black sections, sea-green scuba mask connected to a snorkel fused with the jawline-style faceplate, a dorsal fin symbol on each side, yellow distinctive eyes, a white torso with a black shark head on the chest and side yellow sections, ultramarine-blue trapezoid shoulders with black vents and both yellow and white parts, ultramarine-blue forearms with a fold-in fin and sea-green wrist rings, white upper arms and upper legs, black hands with claw-based fingers, an ultramarine-blue hip with a yellow-ringed black upper section, ultramarine-blue lower legs with half a submarine hull on each side, sea-green strips on the shins, white calves, black feet with two-pronged silver tips, yellow triangles on the base and a rudder as a heel each, and the back contains a unit comprised of shark motif parts, consisting him upper head, pectoral fins, and a small shark tail with two boosters. His vehicle form is a submarine with a shark head, four pectoral fins, the tail and the submarine sail acting as the dorsal fin.

## Arsenal & Techniques

  * **Metal Card - Deep Trident** (메탈카드 - 딥 트라이던트, _Metal Kadeu - Dib Teulaideonteu_): A large rifle with a trident blade serving as the muzzle. It's carried in a holster on Deep Bite's right side when used as a firearm. In close combat, it can be wielded as a spear.
    * **Beam Trident** (빔 트라이던트, _Bim Teulaideonteu_): Deep Bite fires a high-pressure water stream from the Deep Trident's spear blade.
    * **Trident Submarine** (트라이던트 서브마린, _Teulaideonteu Seobeumalin_): Deep Bite generates water at the Deep Trident's muzzle to create a waterspout and throws it at the enemy.
    * **Trident Gatling** (트라이던트 게틀링, _Teulaideonteu Geteulling_): The Deep Trident's blade rotates rapidly to fire water bolts like a machine gun.
    * **Trident Cannon** (트라이던트 캐논, _Teulaideonteu Kaenon_): Deep Bite fires the Deep Trident's trident blade like a harpoon.
  * **Abyss Cannon** (어비스 캐논, _Eobiseu Kaenon_): A firearm with a bayonet wielded exclusively in Deep Bite's left hand. The accuracy is high enough to hit drones from a distance. When not in use, it is stored on the left side of Deep Bite's waist.

## Trivia

  * Deep Bite is the only Metal Cardbot in the series to possess overt animal traits, which revealed that he is a Wild Cardbot.
  * He is the second Cardbot after [Black Hook](</wiki/Black_Hook> "Black Hook") to have a watercraft as his vehicle mode.
  * The Chinese set of Deep Bite contains minor changes: the eyes are colored green and the upper translucent head has a darker blue shade coloration.
  * While retroactively confirmed, Deep Bite is the first Wild Cardbot to appear in the series.
